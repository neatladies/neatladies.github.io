Affiliate UX

The Amazon click comes after the product page.

The homepage should not throw visitors straight to Amazon. It should guide them into a dedicated product page where the decision becomes easier.

01 Discovery from Pinterest or search

The visitor lands on a polished visual page that feels like a curated jewelry edit, not a generic product dump.

02 Dedicated product page

Each item gets its own page with product images, styling notes, who it fits, gift angle, and practical buying context.

03 Intentional Amazon outbound click

The affiliate button appears when the reader already understands the pick and is more likely to continue with purchase intent.

Gift-ready angle

Gift pieces need more than a button.

A product like a cross necklace should have a page that explains the emotional angle, presentation, styling, and who it suits before the reader reaches Amazon.

Show the presentation clearly Gift buyers care about how the piece feels when received, not just how it looks in a product thumbnail.
Explain the styling use Keep it editorial and visual: outfits, occasions, everyday wear, and gift situations.
Keep the CTA honest Neatladies does not sell the product directly. The final buying action happens on Amazon.
